About the application:
The virtual guide to the Modern Architecture Roadmap for the city of Tomar integrates a selected set of public and residential buildings which were designed between 1930 and 1965.
The Modern Architecture Roadmap emerged as a result of a research project started at the end of 2015, and for which it was established a protocol between the Polytechnic Institute of Tomar and the Tomar City Council. The partnership enabled the development of a study on Modern Architecture in the city of Tomar, through the survey, research and analysis of a set of buildings, designed between 1930 and 1965. The work developed resulted in a exhibition and printed publication of a script in book form, in 2018.
Since one of the objectives of the project focused on the dissemination of this heritage to the general public, it was considered relevant to its transposition to other formats and platforms. Indeed, the constitution of a virtual guide can prove to be an important basis. for the valorization of buildings built under the aegis of modernism and the urban spaces they occupy and transform. The virtual registry of immovable object can thus be a way to frame and expand the strategies for the dissemination of architectural heritage to users increasingly frequent digital platforms, creating new references as an alternative to cultural guides and itineraries and conventional tourist attractions.
The Modern Architecture Roadmap virtual guide was developed by students of the Project course unit (year academic 2018-2019) of the Degree in Computer Engineering, of the Polytechnic Institute of Tomar.
